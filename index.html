Legal Framework

Ghana's Narcotics
Cultivation Law

The Narcotic Control Commission Act 2020 (Act 1019) establishes a clear, modern regulatory architecture for industrial hemp cultivation in Ghana — providing the legal certainty that institutional and private investors require.

⚖️
The Enabling Legislation
Act 1019, enacted by the Parliament of Ghana in 2020, expressly legalises the cultivation, processing, and export of industrial hemp under a licensing regime. It represents a landmark shift in national agricultural policy.
🪪
Licensing Authority
The Narcotic Control Commission (NACOC) is the sole licensing authority for industrial hemp. It issues cultivation permits, conducts field inspections, and enforces THC concentration limits to ensure operators remain within lawful parameters.
🔬
Industrial Hemp Definition
Under Act 1019, industrial hemp is defined as Cannabis sativa L. varieties with a THC content at or below the legally prescribed threshold — distinguishing it clearly from controlled narcotic cannabis.
🌍
Regional Leadership
Ghana is among the first West African nations to establish a formal industrial hemp licensing framework, positioning licensed operators to capture export demand from neighbouring markets where domestic cultivation remains prohibited.
📋
Compliance Obligation
Hempwealth Ghana operates with full NACOC licensing compliance, maintaining audit-ready records, verified seed provenance documentation, and ongoing THC testing protocols across all cultivation phases.
